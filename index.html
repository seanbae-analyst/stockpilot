💡 Valuation helps you judge if a stock is cheap or expensive. Think of it like checking if a house is fairly priced before buying.
P/E Ratio
?
💡 P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings) — How much you're paying for $1 of the company's profit. P/E of 20 means you pay $20 for every $1 they earn. Lower = cheaper. Average S&P 500 is ~22.

P/B Ratio
?
💡 P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book) — How much you're paying vs what the company actually owns (assets minus debts). Under 1 = potentially undervalued. Over 3 = priced for growth.

EV/EBITDA
?
💡 EV/EBITDA — Enterprise Value divided by earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation. A better P/E that works even for companies with debt. Under 10 = cheap, over 20 = expensive.

DCF Fair Value
?
💡 DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) — A model that estimates what a stock SHOULD be worth based on future cash flows. If fair value > current price, the stock may be undervalued.

💡 Risk Analysis — Every investment has risk. This tab helps you understand HOW RISKY this specific stock is, so you can decide if you're comfortable holding it.
Beta
?
💡 Beta — Measures how much this stock moves compared to the overall market. Beta of 1 = moves same as market. Beta of 2 = twice as volatile. Beta of 0.5 = half as volatile. High beta = more risk AND more potential reward.

🧾 Tax Calculator
💡 Capital Gains Tax — When you SELL a stock for a profit, you owe tax on that gain. The rate depends on how long you held it. Short-term (<1 year) = taxed as regular income (up to 37%). Long-term (>1 year) = lower rate (0%, 15%, or 20%). This is US tax — Korean residents may have different rules.
